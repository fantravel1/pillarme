Some Destinations Change How You See the World

Marcus had traveled to forty countries. He'd seen the Northern Lights, walked the Great Wall, stood at Machu Picchu at sunrise. But nothing prepared him for Chad.

Three weeks in the Ennedi Plateau—ancient rock formations rising from the desert like forgotten cathedrals. Camels crossing salt flats that stretched to the horizon. Stars so thick at night they erased any difference between sky and land. When he returned, friends noticed something different. The restlessness was gone. He'd found what he didn't know he was looking for.

Chad remains one of Earth's last true frontiers—a country where the Sahara meets the Sahel, where prehistoric rock art tells stories thousands of years old, and where wildlife roams in numbers that remind you what Africa once was. For travelers who've seen everything else, Chad offers what nowhere else can: the extraordinary.
