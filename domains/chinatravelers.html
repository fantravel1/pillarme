Some Journeys Transform You

David had planned the usual itinerary—Beijing, Shanghai, the Great Wall. Tourist photos. Checkbox travel. Then his guide took him down a narrow alley in Xi'an to a family dumpling shop that had served the same recipe for four generations. No English. No menus. Just a grandmother who smiled and began cooking.

That afternoon changed everything. He extended his trip by two weeks. He learned three words of Mandarin. He sat in temples, walked through villages guidebooks never mentioned, and watched tai chi in parks at dawn. When he returned home, he didn't just have photos—he had stories. He understood something about the world he hadn't before.
