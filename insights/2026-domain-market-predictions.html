Prediction #5: New gTLD Shakeout Continues

Generic TLD Consolidation Selective Only

The hundreds of new generic TLDs introduced in the 2010s continue to struggle for relevance. Most will remain low-value, but select extensions (.io, .co, .xyz, .app) will strengthen their positions in specific niches.

We recommend extreme selectivity‚Äîmost new gTLDs remain poor investments compared to legacy extensions.

Strategic Recommendations

Based on our analysis, here's how different audiences should approach 2026:

For Entrepreneurs & Startups

  • Act now on domain acquisition. Prices will only increase. If you can't afford to buy, rental provides immediate access.
  • Prioritize .com or .ai depending on your industry. These extensions carry the most trust and value.
  • Budget 5-10% of branding costs for domain. This investment pays dividends in credibility and marketing efficiency.

For Established Businesses

  • Audit your domain portfolio. Ensure you own or control variations that competitors could exploit.
  • Consider strategic acquisitions. Category-defining domains will appreciate faster than inflation.
  • Explore rental for expansion domains. Test new markets with premium rented domains before committing capital.

For Domain Investors

  • Focus on quality over quantity. The market is rewarding premium names while generic inventory stagnates.
  • Consider rental yield. Rental income can exceed 10% annually while maintaining asset appreciation.
  • Watch .ai closely. It's the strongest growth opportunity in the market.

The Bottom Line

2026 will be characterized by continued appreciation of premium domains, the mainstream emergence of domain rental, and the consolidation of .ai as the premier alternative to .com. The window for affordable premium domain access‚Äîwhether through purchase or rental‚Äîis closing.

Smart money is moving now. The question isn't whether to invest in premium domains, but how to do it most efficiently for your situation.
